Fernando Gallego Donoso

Fernando Gallego Donoso is a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Málaga and a member of the Computational Intelligence in Biomedicine (ICB) research group. Graduated in Software Engineering and holding a Master's degree in Computer Engineering with a specialization in Data Science from the same university, he has dedicated his research career since 2020 to leveraging artificial intelligence to solve real-world problems. His doctoral thesis focuses on ensuring the energy flexibility of smart grids through AI-driven solutions.

In recent years, he has shifted his research towards natural language processing, particularly in the context of biomedical language, both in Spanish and other under-resourced languages. Currently, his main research line focuses on extracting information from electronic health records. He employs advanced natural language processing techniques, such as the recognition and linking of medical entities to international standards, aiming to improve decision-making in the medical field. His work has contributed to the development of innovative methods for semantic normalization and contextual disambiguation, tackling multilingual challenges and optimizing the integration of these technologies into the healthcare sector.

        Other Mentions

        • On December 3, 2024, I received an honorable mention for the best scientific publication: "Recognition and normalization of multilingual symptom entities using in-domain-adapted BERT models and classification layers." These awards are organized by the University of Málaga and the Multilingual Language Technologies Research Institute.

          In this publication, we worked with representation LLMs based on BERT, automatic translation, and managed to fine-tune only a dense layer for the recognition and normalization of medical entities across multiple languages. This approach achieved significant improvements in languages that lack a fully international version of the SNOMED-CT terminology.
